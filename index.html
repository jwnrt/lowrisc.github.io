  • Open

    Our designs are permissively licensed and developed with collaborators from around the world. We implement the free and open RISC-V ISA.

  • Secure

    We are pursuing novel security features such as tagged memory. Unlike proprietary offerings, all aspects of the design can be fully audited.

  • Flexible

    Hardware needs softening up - through minion cores we enable I/O interfaces and real-time control to be defined in software.

Open to the core - lowRISC is unlocking the potential of open source development for the hardware world

The lowRISC platform aims to be the "Linux of the hardware world", providing a high quality, secure, and open base for derivative designs. We will prove our design with volume silicon manufacture and an accompanying low-cost development board. Our goal is to lower the barrier of entry to producing custom silicon, establishing a vibrant ecosystem around secure and open hardware designs. lowRISC was formed as a not-for-profit community-driven organisation to pursue these aims.

We are hiring for multiple positions to deliver on an exciting, high impact, open source hardware roadmap in collaboration with Google and other industry partners.

    lowRISC 0-6 milestone release

    The lowRISC 0.6 milestone release is now available. We’ve also taken the opportunity to re-organise our documentation, adding an easy to follow quick-start guide.   • January 12, 2018

    lowRISC 0-5 milestone release

    The lowRISC 0.5 milestone release is now available. The various changes are best described in our accompanying documentation, but the main focus is the integration of open-source Ethernet IP. The tutorial demonstrates how to use Ethernet support to boot with an NFS root, as well as with a rootfs on SD card. Our main development focus currently is migrating to a newer version of the upstream Rocket chip design and reintegrating our changes on top of that, but we felt that the integration of Ethernet support merits a release before that change.
